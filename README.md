This project was focused on OSINT investigation using historical sources, archived newspapers, French postal encoding, and open-source research techniques. The objective was to identify a real person connected to a maritime incident in Brittany in 1925 by analysing several clues provided in the challenge files.

The investigation started with an old envelope containing an orange French postal barcode. After researching the structure of French postal sorting codes and comparing the symbols with historical barcode references, I decoded the sequence and determined the postal code as 29760. This helped narrow the geographical area of the investigation to the Brittany region in France.

The next stage involved analysing a damaged newspaper clipping and translating French text from additional notes. By identifying references to Arctic explorer Roald Amundsen and comparing headlines with historical archives of L'Ouest-Éclair, I was able to determine the approximate publication period of the newspaper.

Using archived French newspaper databases and historical maritime records, I located reports describing a fishing boat disaster that occurred on 23 May 1925 near Audierne, Brittany. Further research on regional historical archive websites allowed me to identify the full crew list involved in the incident.

Based on the contextual clues from the investigation, including the reference to the youngest crew member who was under driving age, I identified Yves-Marie Gourlaouen, aged 15, as the correct individual connected to the event.

This project helped strengthen practical OSINT skills including:

historical archive research,
multilingual information gathering,
barcode and pattern decoding,
correlation of fragmented evidence,
timeline reconstruction,
validation of information from multiple open sources.

The investigation also demonstrated the importance of patience, structured research methodology, and cross-referencing historical data during open-source intelligence investigations.
